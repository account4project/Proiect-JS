Evolution

The BlueBeryl Book Club has been around since 2005, when it was inaugurated in the scope of being an opportunity of monthly meet-up between friends.
Since then, it has gathered a complex history - close to all of the original founders have quit, but the club's popularity has only grown. Today, there are quite
several subdivisions, and the meetings have become extremely frequent: multiple times a week, across the entirety of NYC, and currently there are talks of
expanding to other cities as well.

Founders

Emma (me!), Alex, Khalid, Charlotte and Will are the original founders. All of us have been avid readers since forever, and it has always been a discussion
subject. In 2007 Alex and Charlotte quit, for they couldn't spare the time anymore due to their busy career lives, and in 2013 Will had to stop coming after
moving away. Only I and Khalid remain, but it's significantly less gloomy than it sounds - we've had many members offer to help and manage the meetings,
trying to find a solution around everyone's busy schedules.

Present

Currently, there are 9 individual subgroups, each centered around specific genres and with their own style of discussion and debate. Albeit our taste might differ,
many of our members share a common love for books. We hold yearly votes for the best book of the year, with the winner of 2019 being The Testaments by Margaret
Atwood, and the winner of last year Educated by Tara Westover. Our clubs frequent many places, some of our favourites being The elk, Little Collins & Hi-Collar.
We have over 70 frequent members and up to 150 less active ones (mostly due to their busy lives).
